A novel tool for spectroscopic inference of fundamental stellar parameters

How can we leverage the wealth of high quality synthetic stellar libraries to estimate fundamental stellar parameters? We have developed a novel approach for making accurate and unbiased inferences of fundamental stellar parameters (e.g., effective temperature, surface gravity, metallicity) from spectroscopic observations.

The forward-modeling formalism we have developed is generic (easily adaptable to data from any instrument or covering any wavelength range) and modular, in that it can incorporate external prior knowledge or additional data (e.g., broadband photometry) and account for instrumental and non-stellar effects on the spectrum (e.g., parametric treatments of extinction, spots, etc.). We use an approach inspired by Gaussian processes that employs adaptive correlated noise to account for systematic discrepancies between the observations and the synthetic spectral library. This ensures that issues like uncertainties in atomic or molecular constants do not strongly bias the parameter inferences. In addition to extracting a set of unbiased inferences of the (posterior) probability distributions for basic stellar parameters, this approach "maps" out problematic spectral regions in the synthetic libraries that could be used as a basis for improving the models. As a demonstration, we present some preliminary results from modeling optical spectra of well-characterized exoplanet host stars and nearby pre-main sequence stars.

A open-source code suite that implements this approach in Python is under development and will be released soon.

Truncated Disks in TW Hya Association Multiple Star Systems

In 2009, I worked with Sean as an SAO REU student to study the HD 98800 quadruple star system. The unique architecture of this stellar system enabled us to test theories of tidal disk truncation on two scales. Our work resulted in the following paper and AAS poster, which won the Chambliss Student Achievemnt Award.

Truncated Disks in TW Hya Association Multiple Star Systems
Andrews, Sean M.; Czekala, Ian; Wilner, D. J.; Espaillat, Catherine; Dullemond, C. P.; Hughes, A. M.
The Astrophysical Journal, Volume 710, Issue 1, pp. 462-469 (2010)
